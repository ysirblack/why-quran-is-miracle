## Main Stress Points

### 1. Part 5 takes the hardest damage

All three runs agree that Part 5 is the least stable zone.

- In the baseline run, it still leaned positive.
- In the hostile-but-fair run, it fell to `MIXED`.
- In the maximum-pressure naturalist run, it stayed `MIXED`.

This means the protocol does **not** need Part 5 to be uniformly strong in order to keep its overall direction, but it also means Part 5 is the first place a critic will press.

### 2. Part 2 survives only after discipline

Part 2 survives the harsher runs, but no longer as an easy “miracle wall.”

- cluster discipline matters
- weaker patterns need demotion
- convergence survives better than individual flashy claims

The harsh runs do not destroy Part 2, but they force it into a more disciplined evidential role.

### 3. Parts 1 and 3 are the most stable

The most stable sections across all runs are:

- Part 1: preservation + challenge
- Part 3: carrier profile

These remained `YES` even under maximum-pressure naturalist scrutiny.

### 4. Parts 4, 6, 8, and 9 soften before they break

These sections do not collapse in the harsher runs, but they lose their clean edge:

- Part 4 becomes “strong historical pressure” rather than impossibility
- Part 6 becomes “comparative advantage” rather than total uniqueness proof
- Part 8 becomes “human models remain underbuilt” rather than mechanically dead
- Part 9 becomes “best fit” rather than “closed proof”

## Final Comparison

### Baseline

The protocol lands strongly on `d` with broad convergence.

### Hostile but Fair

The protocol still lands on `d`, but only after visible downgrades in Part 2, Part 5, Part 7, and Part 9.

### Maximum-Pressure Naturalist

The protocol still lands on `d`, but now by a narrow edge. The result is no longer “comfortable dominance.” It becomes:

`divine origin remains the strongest surviving explanation available within the protocol, but only after substantial trimming, pressure, and downgrading.`

---

## Overall Takeaway

The comparison suggests three things:

1. The protocol is not trivial to overturn.
2. The protocol is most vulnerable in Part 5 and secondarily in over-extended Part 2 claims.
3. Even after harder skeptical pressure, the overall structure still tends to converge on `d`, though the confidence level narrows significantly.

In short:

- the baseline run says `d`
- the hostile-but-fair run still says `d`
- the maximum-pressure naturalist run still says `d`, but only barely

That is probably the most important stress-test result in the entire comparison.
