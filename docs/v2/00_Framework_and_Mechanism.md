# READ THIS FIRST — MANDATORY BRIEFING

## § 1. What This Protocol Is — And Is Not

This protocol is an **objective, cumulative model-testing framework** using inference to best explanation (IBE) — the same method used by courts, historians, Darwin, detectives, and diagnosticians. It presents evidence, asks questions, and lets YOUR answers determine YOUR conclusion. The protocol does not know the result in advance. You do not know the result in advance. The result emerges from YOUR honest engagement with the evidence.

**This is not an argument. It is a structured evaluation.** Both human-authorship models and divine-origin models are subjected to genuine, rigorous testing. Both sides can gain points. Both sides can lose points. Both sides can be eliminated if they fail enough critical tests. The protocol's only demand is **logical consistency** — that your answers do not contradict each other, and that your conclusion matches your own recorded evidence.

The testing burden falls on BOTH sides, but in different ways:

- **Human-authorship models** face ~50 critical questions testing whether they can explain the observed features (literary excellence, preservation, mathematical structure, scientific foreknowledge, carrier profile, source access, composition constraints)
- **Divine-origin model** faces ~20 critical falsification tests (internal contradictions, scientific errors, historical errors, self-serving content, preservation failures, met challenges, moral degradation, theological incoherence)
- **~39 [DEBATABLE] questions** test BOTH sides simultaneously — these are contested scholarly questions where either model can gain or lose
- **[REVERSE-NAME] questions** apply the same "Can you NAME..." format to the divine model that human models face

**What the protocol does NOT do:**

- It does not tell you what to conclude
- It does not assume any model fails before you answer
- It does not argue "human models fail, therefore God"
- It does not treat any question's outcome as predetermined

**What the protocol DOES do:**

- It enforces consistency: your conclusion must match your tally
- It prevents contradiction: you cannot accept a fact and then ignore its implications
- It demands sincerity: if you score "weakens human" on a binary question, you must either score "strengthens divine" or name a specific third alternative — you cannot park in "neutral" without reason
- It requires you to question your own knowledge, follow logical chains to their conclusions, and be truthful with yourself at every step

**What the protocol ASKS of you:**

- **Learn.** This is not a quiz you pass or fail — it is a research exercise. Many of these questions touch on history, linguistics, manuscript studies, mathematics, and comparative religion. If you do not know enough to answer honestly, research it. Look up the Birmingham manuscript. Read about the isnad system. Check the scholarly debate on the Syriac Alexander Legend. Investigate BOTH sides — not just the side you are already comfortable with.
- **Be sincere.** Sincerity means being willing to follow evidence even when it leads somewhere unexpected. It means admitting "I don't know" when you genuinely don't. It means not dismissing evidence because of where it points, and not accepting evidence just because it confirms what you already believe.
- **Research both sides.** If a question presents an objection against the Qur'an, investigate the objection AND the scholarly response — thoroughly, from primary sources where possible. If a question presents evidence for the Qur'an, investigate the evidence AND the strongest counter-argument. The protocol presents both sides. Your job is to evaluate both sides honestly.

**The evaluation is yours. The result is yours. We built the system. You walk through it. Wherever the evidence leads you — that is your answer.**

## § 1.5 Protocol Self-Validation Principle

A well-designed test must work in BOTH directions:

- If Model A is true → honest answers naturally produce results consistent with Model A
- If Model A is false → the test's falsification mechanisms expose the failure

This protocol applies this principle to BOTH competing models:

**If the divine-origin model is TRUE:**
- Literary excellence, preservation, mathematical structure, scientific foreknowledge, and carrier profile are all EXPECTED outcomes of a divine source
- The DC-1 through DC-15 falsification tests should be DIFFICULT to answer YES to — because the text genuinely has no contradictions, errors, or met challenges
- The protocol naturally produces a result consistent with divine origin

**If the divine-origin model is FALSE:**
- DC-1 through DC-15 should be EASY to answer YES to
- "Can you NAME a verified scientific error?" — you should be able to name one
- "Can you NAME an internal contradiction?" — you should be able to cite one
- "Can you PRODUCE a met literary challenge?" — you should be able to produce one
- The mathematical patterns should be explicable by cherry-picking
- A source or teacher should be identifiable
- The protocol naturally produces a result that eliminates the divine model

**If human-authorship models are TRUE:**
- The ~50 critical questions testing human models should be answerable
- A teacher, source, or mechanism should be nameable
- Mathematical patterns should appear in comparison texts (Bible, Mahabharata)
- The protocol naturally produces a result consistent with human authorship

**If human-authorship models are FALSE:**
- The ~50 critical NAME/PRODUCE/CITE questions should be unanswerable
- No teacher, source, or mechanism should be documentable
- Cross-text tests should produce zero comparable patterns
- The protocol naturally produces a result that eliminates human models

**The principle:** This protocol does not pre-assign the result. It exposes whether your own answers are consistent with the model you prefer — or whether your answers lead somewhere your preferences did not expect. The protocol's only function is to ensure CONSISTENCY: that you do not accept a fact in one question and ignore its implications in the next.

**The test of this principle:** If the divine-origin model is false, DC-1 through DC-15 should be answerable with real examples. If human-authorship models are true, the protocol's ~50 critical NAME/PRODUCE/CITE questions should not remain unanswered. If neither set yields the expected answers — that is data about reality, not about protocol design. See DC-1 through DC-15 in Part 9C and FA1-FA20 in Part 10C.

# PROTOCOL INTERPRETATION NOTE

This protocol is a cumulative comparative test, not a trivia quiz and not a collection of isolated miracle claims.

Its purpose is to present evidence from multiple domains and let YOU evaluate whether the data is better explained by human-authorship models, by the text's own claim about its source, or by some other explanation. The protocol does not predetermine the outcome. It structures the inquiry so that your reasoning is consistent, your evidence is examined, and your conclusion follows from your own answers — not from the protocol's design.

For that reason, this protocol must not be read question-by-question in isolation. Some questions are weighted because they materially affect model viability. Some questions are foundational because they establish constraints that must be carried forward. Some questions are debatable because genuine scholarly disagreement exists — and both sides can score.

A respondent is not required to accept every individual claim as decisive. But a respondent must remain logically consistent:

- if a foundational constraint is accepted, later answers must respect it unless explicitly overturned;
- if a fact is accepted, its bearing on the tested model must also be addressed;
- if neutrality is chosen on an [ISR-LINKED] question, a specific third alternative must be named;
- if a Cascading Gate is locked, subsequent Parts must respect that Gate.

This protocol does not eliminate all disagreement. It does, however, distinguish serious disagreement from low-quality evasion.

### GATE LOCK ENFORCEMENT SCALE (GLES)

**The Problem:** Without mechanical binding, a respondent can answer 40+ questions that overwhelmingly weaken human models, then lock the gate as "MIXED" — contradicting their own recorded answers. This is diplomatic hedging, not honest evaluation. The GLES prevents this.

**Step 1 — Sub-Claim Decomposition:**

Each Gate statement is split into component sub-claims. The respondent evaluates EACH sub-claim independently based on their answers to the questions that tested it.

| Part | Gate Sub-Claims                                                                                                                                                           |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | SC1: Extraordinary literary quality / SC2: Preserved textual integrity / SC3: Literarily supreme (reference point of Arabic) / SC4: Imitation challenge unmet 1,400 years |
| 2    | SC1: Contains mathematically precise structural patterns / SC2: Patterns not found in other texts                                                                         |
| 3    | SC1: Carrier was illiterate / SC2: Carrier was sincere / SC3: Behavior incompatible with fraud / SC4: Biographical record transmitted through most rigorous methodology   |
| 4    | SC1: No plausible source access / SC2: No documented composition mechanism                                                                                                |
| 5    | SC1: Contains scientifically accurate information / SC2: Contains historically accurate information / SC3: Information impossible for 7th-century human                   |
| 6    | SC1: No other text matches mathematical profile / SC2: No other tradition matches transmission profile / SC3: No other text matches combined profile                      |
| 7    | SC1: Strongest objections examined at full strength / SC2: None constitutes a falsifying result                                                                           |
| 8    | SC1: All individual human models tested / SC2: No model survives all accumulated evidence                                                                                 |
| 9    | SC1: Divine origin is not merely last option standing / SC2: It positively predicts observed features                                                                     |
| 10   | SC1: Protocol operated fairly / SC2: Data is robust / SC3: Conclusion is warranted                                                                                        |

For each sub-claim, the respondent marks: **YES / NO / INSUFFICIENT DATA**

**Step 2 — Gate Verdict Derivation (mandatory — not optional):**

The gate verdict is DERIVED from the sub-claim pattern. The respondent does not choose freely.

| Sub-Claim Pattern                                   | Required Gate Verdict                                                        |
| --------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| All sub-claims YES                                  | **YES** (mandatory)                                                          |
| Majority YES, zero NO (some INSUFFICIENT DATA)      | **MOSTLY YES** (mandatory)                                                   |
| Genuine split: at least one YES and at least one NO | **MIXED** (allowed — must specify which sub-claims are YES and which are NO) |
| Majority NO, zero YES                               | **MOSTLY NO** (mandatory)                                                    |
| All sub-claims NO                                   | **NO** (mandatory)                                                           |

**MIXED is ONLY available when sub-claims genuinely split** — at least one sub-claim YES and at least one sub-claim NO. If all sub-claims are YES but the respondent "feels" it should be MIXED, that is hedging, not evaluation.

**Step 3 — Part-Level Consistency Check (mandatory):**

After deriving the gate verdict, the respondent must complete this consistency check:

```

═══════════════════════════════════════

        OR revise the gate verdict to match your recorded data.
```

### ANTI-HEDGING RULES

**Rule GH-1 (MIXED Restriction):**
MIXED is prohibited unless the respondent identifies a SPECIFIC sub-claim rated NO with SPECIFIC question numbers supporting that rating. "I just feel it's mixed" or "there are arguments on both sides" is not valid. "SC2 (preservation) is NO because my answer to P1-39 (qira'at variants) showed X" IS valid.

**Rule GH-2 (Strong Spread Override):**
If Part-local Weighted Spread > 20 in one direction AND all CRITICAL questions in the Part went in that same direction AND zero critical hits for the opposing side → the gate verdict must be at minimum **MOSTLY YES** (or MOSTLY NO if spread favors human). MIXED is mechanically prohibited under these conditions.

**Rule GH-3 (No Diplomatic Compression):**
A respondent cannot soften a gate verdict because of general discomfort if their recorded scores are strongly directional. This is LRP-13 (conclusion must match tally) applied at Part level. If your answers produced a strong result, your gate must reflect that strong result. Discomfort is not evidence.

**Rule GH-4 (Gate-Tally Divergence = Forced Revision):**
If the derived gate verdict contradicts the Part-local tally direction (e.g., tally is strongly pro-divine but gate is MIXED or NO), the respondent MUST either:
(a) Revise specific answers to bring the tally in line with the gate, OR
(b) Revise the gate to match the tally

The respondent cannot proceed to the next Part with an unresolved gate-tally divergence. This is a hard stop.

| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------ |
| **YES**        | All sub-claims confirmed by respondent's answers | All sub-claims YES                         |

**Gate Dependency:** Each Part's Gate statement depends on prior Gates. If Part 1's Gate is NO (text is not preserved), Part 2's mathematical patterns lose their evidential force (patterns in a corrupted text could be post-hoc). The protocol tracks these dependencies explicitly.

# SCORING SYSTEM

## 1. WEIGHTED SCORING

YES and NO do not have a fixed direction. A YES to "Can you NAME a teacher?" strengthens the human model. A YES to "Is the Qur'an preserved?" strengthens the divine model. The direction depends on the question, not the answer.

The protocol tracks TWO weighted model tallies. For each answer, record whether it strengthens, weakens, or is neutral toward each model. The weight depends on the question's classification:

- **CRITICAL question** = effect counts **×3**
- **Regular question** = effect counts **×1**
- **[DEBATABLE] question** = effect counts **×1** (but BOTH sides can gain or lose)
- **Neutral** = 0 (regardless of weight)
- **[PROCEDURAL]** = forced 0 for both models

## 2. WHAT MAKES A QUESTION CRITICAL

A question is [CRITICAL] if one answer materially threatens or materially supports the **viability** of an entire explanatory model — not merely its plausibility.

Two types:

- **Binary-Critical:** questions containing verbs like NAME, PRODUCE, CITE, IDENTIFY, or demanding specific documented evidence. PARTIALLY is forbidden on these. YES requires a named, evidenced instance.
- **Evaluative-Critical:** all other CRITICAL questions. PARTIALLY is allowed only with explicit justification and cannot be used as default parking.

## 3. FOUNDATIONAL CONSTRAINT CARRY-FORWARD

Questions marked [FOUNDATIONAL] establish binding interpretive constraints that carry forward into ALL subsequent evaluations. Once active, the constraint applies to ALL subsequent questions unless explicitly overturned.

Foundational constraints do NOT auto-add points to later questions. They constrain how later questions must be interpreted. This prevents double counting while ensuring logical consistency.

## 4. MODEL ELIMINATION THRESHOLDS — REBALANCED

**V1 used:** 3 critical hits = elimination for BOTH sides. Critics said "too easy to eliminate human model."

**V2 uses proportional elimination thresholds — and the numbers speak for themselves:**

| Side                   | Critical Tests It Faces    | Hits Needed to Eliminate | Failure Rate Required | Difficulty          |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------- | ------------------------ | --------------------- | ------------------- |
| Human-authorship model | **~50** critical exposures | **30 critical hits**     | **60%**               | Harder to eliminate |
| Divine-origin model    | **~20** critical exposures | **4 critical hits**      | **20%**               | Easier to eliminate |

**Read those numbers carefully.**

To eliminate the human-authorship model, you need **30 out of ~50** critical tests to go against it — a 60% failure rate. The human model can fail on 29 critical questions and STILL survive.

To eliminate the divine-origin model, you need only **4 out of ~20** critical tests to go against it — a 20% failure rate. The divine model can only fail on 3 before it is eliminated.

**The math:**

- The divine model is held to a **3× STRICTER** percentage threshold than the human model (20% vs 60%)
- The human model gets a 60% cushion. The divine model gets a 20% cushion.
- Neither side is favored. Both sides face genuine tests. Both sides CAN be eliminated.

**Why these numbers?**

- **20% for divine:** A claim of divine origin is a strong claim. Strong claims should be held to high standards. If even 4 out of 20 critical tests reveal genuine problems, that should be enough to seriously damage the claim. We are not protecting this model. We are testing it.
- **60% for human:** Human explanations are naturally complex and can partially explain many things. A human model should not be eliminated just because it struggles on a few points. It should be eliminated only when a clear majority of critical tests go against it.

**We do not know the result.** We set these thresholds because they are fair — not because we expect either side to win or lose. Maybe the human model survives everything. Maybe the divine model gets eliminated at test 4. Maybe both survive. Maybe both get eliminated. We do not know, and the protocol does not care. It is a measurement tool, not an argument.

**Whatever the result is — whether it is comfortable for you or not — that is the result.** The protocol does not exist to make you feel good about any conclusion. It exists to make sure your conclusion is honest, consistent, and earned through evidence. If the evidence points somewhere you did not expect, that is not a flaw in the protocol. That is the protocol working.

## 5. INFERENTIAL SYMMETRY RULE (ISR)

**The Problem V1 Had:** Skeptical respondents answer "NO, I cannot name a teacher" but then score the divine model as "neutral" instead of "strengthens." They accept the evidence weakens human authorship but refuse to acknowledge it strengthens the alternative.

**The V2 Solution — Inferential Symmetry Rule (ISR):**

When a question tests a feature that has ONLY TWO possible explanations (human-produced vs. not-human-produced), and the respondent's answer weakens one explanation, the other explanation is LOGICALLY strengthened unless the respondent proposes a **specific third alternative.**

**ISR applies when:**

- The question tests a binary: "did a human do this, or not?"
- The respondent's answer weakens the human explanation
- No specific third alternative is proposed

**ISR does NOT apply when:**

- Multiple human models remain live (weakening Model 1 doesn't strengthen divine if Models 2-6 still work)
- The respondent names a specific third alternative (e.g., "not divine, but an unknown natural mechanism — here's what I mean: \_\_\_")
- The question is [DEBATABLE] or [PROCEDURAL]

**ISR Tag:** [ISR-LINKED] — applied to questions where human and divine are the only two categories in play.

**ISR in practice:** On [ISR-LINKED] questions, if you score "weakens human," you must either score "strengthens divine" OR name a specific alternative explanation in the ISR check field. "Neutral" is not available without a named alternative.

## 6. CAPS — RAISED FOR GENEROSITY

- **UNKNOWN cap: 15%** of total questions (raised from 10% in V1). More generous, still prevents parking.
- **PARTIALLY cap: 30%** of total questions (raised from 20% in V1). More generous, still prevents mass-neutral evasion.

## 7. DOMINANCE SCALE (Dₓ)

The Dominance Scale standardizes how cumulative results are described at checkpoints and at the final verdict. It prevents vague wording from understating or overstating the actual result.

**Primary Metrics:**

- **HC** (Human Collapse Score) = abs(Human model tally)
- **DS** (Divine Support Score) = Divine model tally
- **WS** (Weighted Spread) = DS + HC
- **CA** (Critical Asymmetry) = Human critical hits − Divine critical hits
- **CHE** (Critical Hit Exposure) = Total critical questions per side

**Dominance Bands:**

| Band | Label                           | WS      | CA    | Models Eliminated       | Permitted Language                                 |

| D₀   | Indeterminate                   | <50     | <5    | 0                       | "No directional result"                            |
| D₁   | Mild directional lean           | 50-150  | 5-15  | 0                       | "Directional advantage visible"                    |
| D₂   | Strong asymmetry                | 150-300 | 15-30 | 0-1 weakened            | "Serious explanatory pressure"                     |
| D₃   | Dominant surviving model        | 300-500 | 30-50 | ≥1 eliminated           | "Dominant surviving explanation"                   |

**Threshold Calibration Note (V2):** This protocol contains **527 questions** (113 CRITICAL at ×3 weight, 414 regular at ×1 weight). Maximum weighted score per side = 753. Theoretical WS ceiling ≈ 1,506. Thresholds are calibrated so that D₅ requires >53% of theoretical maximum — meaning ~80% of all scoring questions must go in one direction. **What D₅ means:**

If your own honest answers produced this result, that result deserves to be stated plainly — not softened, not hedged, not buried under qualifiers.

**D₅ is a different level.**

The protocol corrected its own false positives, demoted its own weak claims, and held the divine model to a stricter threshold than the human model — and the result still landed here. At this level, the remaining question is not "is the evidence sufficient?" — the evidence has spoken across 400+ questions. | Tag              | Weight   | Meaning                                      | Rules                                                                                                   |

**Tag Combinations:** A question may carry multiple tags. Example: [CRITICAL] [ISR-LINKED] [CODE-DEPENDENT] means ×3 weight, ISR applies, and code must be run. Each tag operates on its own layer.

**A note on [REVERSE-NAME] difficulty:**

These questions test the divine model with the same binary format used for human models. Critics may observe that answering YES to questions like "Can you NAME a verified scientific error in the Qur'an?" appears practically very difficult. This observation is correct — and it is itself data.

If the divine model were genuinely flawed, these questions would be EASY to answer. A real scientific error would be nameable. A real internal contradiction would be citable. A real met literary challenge would be producible. The difficulty of answering YES is not a flaw in the test design — it is the test's result reflecting the model's performance under 1,400 years of adversarial scrutiny.

The protocol does not prevent a YES answer. It invites it. If you CAN name a verified scientific error, a proven historical falsehood, or a recognized successful response to the literary challenge — do so, with specific evidence. The divine model takes a critical hit. That is how the test works.

The question is not "why are these tests hard?" The question is "why, after 1,400 years of adversarial scholarship, has no one produced a clear YES?"

# THREE-TIER RISK ARCHITECTURE

Questions are classified by their FUNCTION in the protocol:

| Tier                                 | Label       | Function                                                                                                             | Examples                                                    |
| ------------------------------------ | ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tier 1: Core Knockout Set**        | T1-KO, T1-C | Direct divine-model falsification tests. A YES answer here directly threatens divine model survival.                 | DC-1 through DC-7, P1-33, P1-34, P5-31, P5-32               |
| **Tier 2: Extended Adversarial Set** | T2-S, T2-D  | Strongest academic objections, source-dependency tests, moral/comparative questions. Stress-testing zone.            | Part 7B debatable questions, Part 6D theological comparison |
| **Tier 3: Open Challenge Window**    | T3          | Filtered new objections meeting admission standards: specific, evidence-based, capable of surviving expert scrutiny. | Part 7C open investigation                                  |

**Additional labels:**

- **NC-C** (Natural Collapse - Critical): Questions that test human-authorship models with model-viability force
- **APP-P** (Appendix - Peripheral): Logged but not weighted in the core case
- **PROC** (Procedural): Framework questions with no model effect
- **FP-CLEARED**: Investigated for false positive, confirmed NOT false positive
- **FP-DEMOTED**: Investigated for false positive, downgraded (evidence insufficient for core weight)
- **RT-REFUTED**: Red Team attack researched and disproven (strengthens the protocol)

**Knockout Hit vs Critical Hit:**

- A **Knockout Hit (KO)** is a failure serious enough to DIRECTLY threaten divine model survival. Reserved for Tier 1 items only.
- A **Critical Hit** seriously damages a model (×3 weight) but is not automatically a knockout.
- NOT every [CRITICAL] question produces a knockout. Only T1-KO items can produce knockouts.

**Clarification: [DEBATABLE] tag vs T2-D/T2-S tier label:**

These are DIFFERENT systems operating on different layers:

- **[DEBATABLE]** is a SCORING TAG — it means "both sides can gain or lose on this question." It affects HOW the answer is scored (×1 weight, bidirectional).
- **T2-D** is a TIER LABEL — it means the question's FUNCTION is "debatable-level adversarial stress test."
- **T2-S** is a TIER LABEL — it means the question's FUNCTION is "serious-level adversarial stress test."

A question CAN be [DEBATABLE] (scoring) AND T2-S (tier) simultaneously. This means: "both sides can score on this question AND it is a serious stress test." Example: P7B-7 (Syriac Alexander Legend) is [DEBATABLE] because scholars genuinely disagree, but T2-S because it is one of the most important objections the protocol faces.

The rule: [DEBATABLE] does NOT automatically mean T2-D. A debatable question that is especially important, evidentially heavy, or represents a major attack vector may be classified as T2-S despite being scored as [DEBATABLE].

**Full question-level labels are documented in the Label Sheet (docs/v2/17_Protocol_V3_Label_Sheet.md).**

# EVIDENCE CLASSIFICATION: CORE vs AUXILIARY (Eₓ BAND SYSTEM)

Not all evidence in this protocol carries equal weight. The protocol classifies evidence into 5 strength bands (Eₓ) — modeled on the Dominance Scale (Dₓ) but measuring a different dimension.

**What Eₓ measures vs. what Tier labels measure:**

The Tier system (T1-KO, NC-C, T2-S, T2-D, APP-P) classifies a question's **FUNCTION** — what role it plays in the protocol's structure. The Eₓ system classifies the **STRENGTH** of the evidence a question produces. These are different layers that work together:

| Eₓ Band                | Typical Tier        | Meaning                                                              |
| ---------------------- | ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| E₅ (Foundational Lock) | NC-C or T1-KO       | Strongest evidence either direction — can lock or break a Gate alone |
| E₄ (Core Evidence)     | NC-C or strong T2-S | Robust evidence that carries the main case                           |
| E₃ (Strong Support)    | T2-S                | Real pattern, strengthens but doesn't independently lock             |
| E₂ (Auxiliary)         | T2-D                | Interesting, methodology-dependent or partially contested            |
| E₁ (Curiosity)         | APP-P or APP-N      | Noted but not scored for weight                                      |

**When used:** The Eₓ system is a RETROSPECTIVE evaluation tool, applied at the END of the protocol in Part 10 — after all questions are answered and all gates locked. The respondent sees all evidence first, then classifies. Band assignments listed below are STARTING POINTS — the respondent reclassifies based on their own answers throughout Parts 1-9.

## RESILIENCE CLAUSE — What Happens If Evidence Falls? (BOTH DIRECTIONS)

**If an E₅ lock weakening the human model falls:**

Example: a teacher is documented, or an editing trace is found.

- This DAMAGES some arguments (complete isolation, zero external input)
- But it does NOT automatically rebuild a full human-authorship model
- The question SHIFTS: how did THAT teacher produce this text? Where did they get 7-language access? Why no co-author footprint from 23 years of public delivery?
- The skeptic must still account for ALL remaining evidence across ALL bands
- Claims emerging 1,400 years after the fact — after centuries of motivated searching — require extraordinarily strong evidence. Late ≠ false, but late = more caution.

**If an E₅ hit lands AGAINST the divine model:**

Example: a verified scientific error is found, or the literary challenge is met.

- This is a CRITICAL HIT at ×3 weight against the divine model
- The divine model has a 20% elimination threshold — only 4 hits needed
- A perfection claim (divine origin) is LESS resilient to individual failures than a complexity claim (human authorship). This is by design.
- We do NOT apply the "question just shifts" logic here equally. A text claiming divine perfection that contains a verified error has a MORE severe problem than a human-authorship model that loses one source candidate.
- This asymmetry is FAIR: stronger claims carry stricter burdens. The divine model chose to claim perfection. That claim is testable. If it fails, it fails hard.

**The principle:** Both sides can take E₅ hits. But the CONSEQUENCES are asymmetric because the CLAIMS are asymmetric. A human model failing one test is expected (humans are imperfect). A divine model failing one test contradicts its core premise (divine perfection). This asymmetry is not bias — it is the natural consequence of the models' own claims.

**Cluster accounting extension:** This same asymmetry extends to how evidence families are counted. A fully symmetric cluster rule would collapse related objections into fewer anti-divine clusters — reducing the hit count against the divine model. This protocol deliberately does NOT take that easing step. Evidence supporting the divine model is cluster-disciplined (dependent sublayers counted as one family). Objections against the divine model are scored individually at full weight. If the claim is divine origin, it must survive maximum pressure. See Part 2 Statistical Addendum, Rule 8.

# LOCKED RESPONSE PROTOCOL (LRP) — UPDATED FOR V2

All V1 LRP rules are preserved with the following updates:

**LRP-1:** Every question receives exactly one of six responses: YES, MOSTLY YES, PARTIALLY, MOSTLY NO, NO, or UNKNOWN.

**LRP-2:** Once an answer is given, it cannot be retroactively changed without explicit invocation of LRP-12 (Self-Correction Protocol).

**LRP-3:** Every answer must include the full scorecard. No exceptions.

**LRP-4:** UNKNOWN is capped at 15% of total questions (raised from 10%).

**LRP-5:** PARTIALLY is capped at 30% of total questions (raised from 20%).

**LRP-6:** Binary-Critical questions (NAME/PRODUCE/CITE/IDENTIFY) cannot receive PARTIALLY. Answer is YES (with evidence) or NO.

**LRP-7:** Evaluative-Critical PARTIALLY requires explicit justification beyond "both sides have points."

**LRP-8:** Foundational constraints, once active, apply to ALL subsequent questions.

**LRP-9:** PROCEDURAL questions are forced neutral — they do not constitute evidence.

**LRP-10:** Model effects (strengthens/weakens/neutral) must be assigned independently for each model.

**LRP-11:** Final conclusion must be one of:
(a) Ordinary human authorship sufficiently explains the data
(b) Human-authorship models explain some but not all
(c) No tested human-authorship model sufficiently explains the data
(d) Divine origin is the strongest surviving explanation
(e) No conclusion possible

**LRP-12:** Self-Correction Protocol: A respondent may change a prior answer by naming the specific answer, stating the revision, and explaining why. The running tally is recalculated.

**LRP-13:** Conclusion must match tally. If the weighted tally shows human deeply negative and divine strongly positive, choosing conclusion (a) contradicts recorded answers. Identify which answers you revise, or accept the tally's direction.

**LRP-14:** Mini-checkpoints at each Part boundary (replacing V1's 3 major checkpoints with 10 Part-boundary checkpoints).

**LRP-15:** Framework Challenge Window: Limited framework-level objections may be logged before the run, but full methodological evaluation belongs in Part 10.

**LRP-16:** Consistency Rule — the same evaluative standard must be applied to ALL texts, traditions, and claims. If your standard for the Qur'an would also invalidate the Bible, Torah, Vedas, or Iliad, state that. If you apply a stricter standard to one text than to others, explain why.

**LRP-17:** Bare possibility is not sufficient to override directional evidence. "Maybe" requires a specific mechanism, not just logical conceivability.

**LRP-18:** Running tally must be updated after EVERY answer, not just at checkpoints.

**LRP-19:** Model elimination is checked at every Part boundary. If a model has accumulated enough critical hits, it is eliminated at the next checkpoint.

**LRP-20:** Post-run challenges must identify specific rule/question failures, not bare retreats.

**LRP-21:** The Cascading Gate at each Part boundary is binding. It may be overturned only by identifying the specific answers that contradict it.

**LRP-22:** Neutrality on CRITICAL or FOUNDATIONAL questions requires a reasoned counter-explanation, not bare possibility. (Kept from V1.)

**LRP-23 (NEW):** [ISR-LINKED] questions require ISR compliance. Scoring "weakens human" + "neutral divine" without naming a third alternative is an invalid scorecard.

**LRP-24 (NEW):** [DECISION-TREE] questions must follow the branching logic. Skipping to a terminal answer without traversing the intermediate branches is invalid.

**LRP-25 (NEW):** [DEBATABLE] questions must be evaluated at scholarly depth. Surface dismissals ("that's just wrong") without engaging the presented evidence and counter-evidence are invalid.

**LRP-26 (NEW):** [REVERSE-NAME] questions apply the same binary rules as NAME questions. YES requires specific, cited evidence. "I think there might be one" is NO.

**LRP-27 (NEW):** Gate dependency tracking: if a prior Gate is locked as NO, the respondent must explicitly state how this affects the current Part's evidential weight. Ignoring a locked Gate is invalid.

**LRP-28 (NEW):** [LOGIC-CHAIN] questions must be followed step by step. Skipping to the final conclusion without engaging each step is invalid. If you reject a step, identify the specific step number and explain your reasoning.

**LRP-29 (NEW — GLES):** Gate verdicts must be derived from the Sub-Claim Decomposition using the Gate Lock Enforcement Scale. Free-choice gate selection is prohibited. The gate verdict is a FUNCTION of the sub-claim evaluations, not an independent judgment.

**LRP-30 (NEW — Anti-Hedging):** MIXED gate verdicts require at least one sub-claim rated YES and at least one rated NO. If all sub-claims are YES, the gate is YES — regardless of the respondent's general comfort level. Hedging is not evaluation.

**LRP-31 (NEW — Gate-Tally Consistency):** If a Part-level gate verdict contradicts the Part-local tally direction, the respondent must resolve the divergence before proceeding — either by revising specific answers or by revising the gate verdict. Unresolved gate-tally divergence is a hard stop.

**LRP-32 (NEW — Do Not Overclaim):** A claim whose result depends on a specific methodology choice (morphological filter, verse-counting convention, or comparison-space definition) cannot serve as a standalone foundational lock. It must be presented WITH its methodology dependency documented. The respondent evaluates the claim UNDER the documented methodology — and is free to note that alternative methodologies would change the result. Claims that survive across MULTIPLE methodology choices are stronger than claims that depend on one.

**LRP-33 (NEW — Positive Model Requirement):** A respondent who claims that a human-authorship model survives must provide a POSITIVE model — not just doubts. A surviving human model must include: (a) a named mechanism, (b) a documented teacher or transmission channel, (c) a timeline, (d) language access explanation, (e) revision method, (f) why no historical trace survives, (g) why no equivalent text exists. Bare doubt is not a model. A model that leaves these fields blank is labeled "underspecified" and cannot count as a working alternative.

**LRP-34 (NEW — Underspecified Model Rule):** If a human-authorship model leaves required fields blank (mechanism, teacher, timeline, language access, revision method), it is labeled "underspecified." An underspecified model cannot serve as the basis for a conclusion. The respondent must either fill the missing fields or acknowledge the model is incomplete.

**LRP-35 (NEW — Devil's Advocate Gate Rule):** Before locking any Gate, the respondent must write the 3 strongest arguments AGAINST locking that Gate at the proposed verdict. This forces genuine adversarial self-examination before each Gate Lock.

**LRP-36 (NEW — Search Space Transparency):** Every mathematical claim must document: how many patterns were tested, how many failures were found, and the effective p-value after multiple-comparison correction. Reporting only the hits without the search space is incomplete.

**LRP-37 (NEW — Hadith-Independence Declaration):** When presenting evidence, explicitly state whether it depends on hadith sources or derives from the text itself. Evidence from the TEXT (mathematical structure, verse-gaps, literary properties) is independent of hadith reliability debates. Evidence from BIOGRAPHY (illiteracy, sincerity, behavior) depends on hadith/sira sources.

# DECISION-TREE FORMAT SPECIFICATION

**The Problem:** Debatable questions are debated because respondents don't follow the FULL logical chain. They make a surface judgment without examining the context, cause, alternative, or historical comparison.

**The Solution:** A branching question format where each answer leads to the next logical question. The respondent follows the chain step by step and reaches the conclusion through THEIR OWN reasoning.

**Format:**

```
[DECISION-TREE] P[X]-[N]: [Root Question]
├── YES → P[X]-[N].1: [Follow-up A]
│   ├── YES → P[X]-[N].1a: [Deeper follow-up]
│   └── NO → P[X]-[N].1b: [Alternative path]
└── NO → P[X]-[N].2: [Follow-up B]
    ├── YES → P[X]-[N].2a: [Deeper follow-up]
    └── NO → P[X]-[N].2b: [Dead end — identify contradiction]
```

**Scoring Rules:**

1. The ROOT question is recorded but not scored
2. INTERMEDIATE answers are recorded and create logical commitments
3. Only TERMINAL answers (marked [SCORED]) contribute to the tally
4. Dead ends (contradictions, inability to cite evidence) force the respondent to revise
5. Each branch is a legitimate logical path — no branch is designed to be impossible
6. The respondent may exit at any branch by providing a specific alternative explanation

# LOGIC-CHAIN FORMAT SPECIFICATION

**The Problem:** Some questions require the respondent to follow a long sequence of logical steps where each step builds on the previous one. Unlike [DECISION-TREE] (which branches into YES/NO paths), a Logic Chain is LINEAR — step 1 leads to step 2 leads to step 3, and the final conclusion depends on accepting or rejecting each step along the way.

**The Solution:** A numbered sequential format where each step is a small, answerable sub-question. The respondent evaluates each step honestly. If they reject a step, they must state WHERE in the chain they disagree and WHY. The final scored answer is the conclusion the respondent reaches at the end of the chain — whether that agrees or disagrees with the chain's direction.

**Format:**

```
[LOGIC-CHAIN] P[X]-[N]: [Topic]

Step 1: [First premise or factual claim] — Do you accept this? YES/NO
Step 2: [Building on Step 1] — If Step 1, then does this follow? YES/NO
Step 3: [Building on Step 2] — If Steps 1-2, then...? YES/NO
...
Step N: [Final logical conclusion from all previous steps]

CHAIN RESULT: If you accepted Steps 1 through N, your conclusion is: ___
BREAK POINT: If you rejected any step, identify which one and explain why.

[SCORED]: Your final evaluation based on the chain.
```

**Scoring Rules:**

1. Individual steps are RECORDED but not independently scored
2. The respondent must engage each step — skipping steps is invalid
3. Only the FINAL conclusion (based on which steps were accepted/rejected) is scored
4. If the respondent rejects a step, they must identify the specific step number and provide reasoning
5. The chain is designed so each step is individually reasonable — disagreement should be specific, not vague
6. The respondent's own chain of reasoning determines their scored answer

**Difference from [DECISION-TREE]:**

| Feature      | [DECISION-TREE]                                 | [LOGIC-CHAIN]                                      |
| ------------ | ----------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Structure    | Branching (YES/NO paths diverge)                | Linear (each step builds on previous)              |
| Best for     | Debatable topics with genuinely different paths | Complex arguments requiring step-by-step reasoning |
| Scoring      | Terminal node at end of a branch                | Final conclusion after full chain                  |
| Disagreement | Take a different branch                         | Identify the break point                           |

**Anticipated Objections to [LOGIC-CHAIN] Format:**

| Objection                                                                  | Response                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Each step is leading / loaded"                                            | Every step is an independently verifiable factual claim or logical inference. "Hieroglyphics died in 394 CE" is not a leading question — it is a documented historical fact. If a step is factually wrong, reject it and cite your evidence. If it is factually correct, accepting it is not being "led" — it is being honest.                                                                                         |
| "You're forcing YES answers"                                               | No. You can answer NO to any step. The only requirement is that you identify WHICH step you reject and explain WHY. The format does not force agreement — it forces specificity. Vague disagreement is what the format prevents, not disagreement itself.                                                                                                                                                              |
| "The conclusion is assumed in the steps"                                   | The conclusion FOLLOWS from the steps. That is how deductive reasoning works. If you accept all premises, the conclusion is logically entailed — that is not a flaw, it is the definition of a valid argument. If you reject the conclusion, you must reject at least one premise. This is basic logic, not manipulation.                                                                                              |
| "This is a manipulation technique"                                         | This is the Socratic method — used since ~400 BCE by Socrates, formalized in Western philosophy, and employed in every courtroom, medical diagnosis, and scientific investigation. Step-by-step reasoning is how rational inquiry works. If you object to step-by-step reasoning itself, you are objecting to the structure of rational thought.                                                                       |
| "I disagree with the overall direction but can't identify a specific step" | Then your disagreement is not with the evidence — it is with where the evidence leads. The protocol respects your right to disagree, but it distinguishes between evidence-based disagreement (rejecting a specific step with reason) and discomfort-based disagreement (rejecting the conclusion despite accepting every step). The former is legitimate. The latter is documented as Pattern 2 (diplomatic hedging). |
