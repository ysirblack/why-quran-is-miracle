## Methodology Notes

### What Was Counted:

**ALL tokens of the specific words** - including all grammatical variants:

- World (الدنيا): The nominal dun'yā with all prefixes

  - Plain: الدنيا
  - With preposition: بالدنيا، والدنيا، للدنيا

- Hereafter (الآخرة): The feminine nominal al-akhirah with all prefixes
  - Plain: الآخرة
  - With preposition: بالآخرة، والآخرة، للآخرة، وبالآخرة، وللآخرة

### What Was Excluded:

**Other forms from the same roots:**

From World root (د-ن-و):

- دَنَا (danā) - verb "to approach" (2 occurrences)
- أَدْنَىٰ (adnā) - "nearer/less/inferior" (12 occurrences)
- دَان (dān) - "near" (1 occurrence)
- دَانِيَة (dāniyat) - "hanging near" (3 occurrences)

From Hereafter root (أ-خ-ر):

- الآخر (al-akhir) - masculine "the last one" (29 occurrences)
- آخَر (ākhar) - "other/another" (70 occurrences)
- الآخرين (al-ākhirīn) - "the last ones" plural (13 occurrences)
- Verbs: أَخَّرَ (to delay), تَأَخَّرَ (to stay behind), etc.

**Total excluded:** 18 other forms from world root, 135+ other forms from hereafter root

### Why This Methodology:

1. **Linguistically sound:**

   - Counts the specific theological terms
   - Includes all grammatical variants (with clitics)
   - Excludes unrelated meanings from same root
   - الدنيا specifically means "the worldly life"
   - الآخرة specifically means "the hereafter"

2. **Theologically significant:**

   - Quran explicitly pairs "this world and the hereafter" as concepts
   - Example: **"And give us good in this world and good in the hereafter"** (2:201)
   - The balance reflects this fundamental duality

3. **Symmetric:**

   - Same rule applied to both sides
   - Both include all clitic prefixes
   - Both count the specific word only
   - No cherry-picking

4. **Comprehensive:**

   - Includes all 115 occurrences of الدنيا
   - Includes all 115 occurrences of الآخرة (with clitics)
   - Nothing excluded arbitrarily
