## Three Balanced Counts

### Method 1: Singulars Only (24:24)

Exclude dual forms ("two men", "two women"):

- Man singular: 24
- Woman singular: 24
- **Result: Perfect 24:24 balance**

**Dual forms excluded:**

- Man: 2:282, 5:23, 16:76, 18:32, 28:15 (5 duals)
- Woman: 2:282, 28:23 (2 duals)

### Method 2: By Verse (25:25)

Count each verse only once (regardless of repetitions):

- Verses with "man": 25
- Verses with "woman": 25
- **Result: Perfect 25:25 balance**

### Method 3: Semantic Roles (23:23) ⭐

Count by semantic role/concept - when multiple instances serve the SAME conceptual role in a verse, count as one:

- Man semantic roles: 23
- Woman semantic roles: 23
- **Result: Perfect 23:23 balance - matches human chromosome pairs!**

**The Rule:** "When multiple occurrences in a verse represent the SAME semantic role/concept, count them as one entity."

**Application to multi-occurrence verses:**

| Verse     | Occurrences | Semantic Analysis                                       | Roles |
| --------- | ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | ----- |
| **39:29** | 3 men       | 1 slave role (2 contrasting examples) + 1 owner role    | **2** |
| **40:28** | 2 men       | Believer (speaker) ≠ Moses (subject) = different roles  | **2** |
| **66:10** | 2 women     | 1 wife role (2 parallel examples of disbelieving wives) | **1** |

**Detailed breakdown:**

- **39:29**: "a man owned by quarreling partners" and "a man belonging to one master" both represent the **slave role** (contrasting the same concept). The owner is a separate role. → 2 semantic entities
- **40:28**: The believing man (speaker) and Moses (the one being defended) serve **different semantic roles** in the narrative. → 2 semantic entities
- **66:10**: Wife of Noah and wife of Lot are **parallel examples of the same concept** (disbelieving wives given as one unified example). → 1 semantic entity

**Calculation:** 24 singulars - 1 (39:29 adjustment) = **23 men**
**Calculation:** 24 singulars - 1 (66:10 adjustment) = **23 women**
