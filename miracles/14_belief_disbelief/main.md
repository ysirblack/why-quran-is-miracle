## Critical Distinction: كَفُور vs كُفُور

### The Voweling Challenge

Arabic voweling (diacritics) is CRITICAL for distinguishing these two forms:

**كَفُور (kafūr):**

- Voweling: Fatha (َ) on first letter, Damma (ُ) on second
- Meaning: Ungrateful, very ungrateful (adjective)
- QAC count: 12 occurrences
- Category: Adjective describing a person's character

**كُفُور (kufūr):**

- Voweling: Damma (ُ) on first letter, Damma (ُ) on second
- Meaning: Disbelief, ingratitude (noun)
- QAC count: 3 occurrences
- Category: Noun describing the concept itself

**Why This Matters:**

- Without diacritics, both look identical: كفور
- Our script initially caught BOTH (15 total)
- After fixing to match only كُفُور (damma-damma): 3 occurrences ✓
- This is why QAC morphological analysis is essential!

### Verification Challenge

This highlights why manual text searching is insufficient:

1. Tanzil Uthmani text has diacritics (good!)
2. But pattern matching requires exact vowel knowledge
3. QAC provides authoritative morphological tagging
4. Our script must match QAC's precision
